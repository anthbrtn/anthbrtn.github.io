Anthony Burton / Platform Cultures / Internet Research

Writing about digital subjectivity, infrastructures of distrust, and contemporary reaction.

Burton's work follows the forms through which networked publics learn to appear, authenticate themselves, and recognize enemies. Rather than isolating misinformation as a content problem, he is interested in the interfaces, temporalities, and vernacular genres that make certain styles of participation feel native or persuasive.

This approach links work on far-right media, memetic figures such as Wojak, appification, digital libertarianism, deplatforming, and platform authentication. It also reflects a background that runs through literature, philosophy, journalism, and software: formal, but not flat; readable, but suspicious of false clarity.
