Media Studies / Amsterdam

Anthony Burton researches authenticity, platform cultures, and the political styles of digital life.

He is a postdoctoral researcher and lecturer in the Media Studies department at the University of Amsterdam. Working across social theory, cultural analysis, and digital methods, he studies the interfaces, temporalities, and vernacular genres through which networked publics learn how to appear, authenticate themselves, and recognize enemies.

Recent work includes Algorithmic Authenticity, writing on cross-platform misinformation and Canada's far-right media ecology, and ongoing research on appification, digital libertarianism, technofascism, and the ordinary aesthetics of participation.
