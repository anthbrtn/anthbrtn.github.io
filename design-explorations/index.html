Anthbrtn.com redesign studies

Three homepage directions using your current content.

These are meant to feel editorial, academic, and a little stranger than a standard faculty page. They all reuse the current biography, navigation, and selected work from your live site.

Closest to the reference sites

01. Editorial

A sparse, confident front page with strong typography, horizontal rules, and a reading-first layout. This is the most direct answer to the pringle.fail and zentralwerkstatt.org mood.

  • Minimal palette, serif headline, academic but not institutional.
  • Selected writing and current research on the front page.
  • Feels like a personal dossier rather than a template.
Open Editorial

Most organized

02. Dossier

A denser grid that treats the site like a research file: current agenda, publications, talks, links, and contact all visible at a glance.

  • Structured cards and labels.
  • More immediately useful for visitors hunting specific material.
  • Still distinctive, but less austere than the references.
Open Dossier

Most expressive

03. Atmosphere

A more dramatic landing page with oversized type, ambient shapes, and a stronger sense of authorship. Still readable, but more willing to feel like an argument.

  • Large statement intro and visual rhythm.
  • Feels contemporary without becoming startup-like.
  • Best if you want the homepage to have more personality.
Open Atmosphere
